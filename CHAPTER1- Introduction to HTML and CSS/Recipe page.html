My Recipe To: Chicken Tikka Masala

More Recipes | Contact

Chicken Tikka Masala is a curried dish with grilled chicken chunks simmered in a flavorful tomato-onion masala sauce. "Tikka" means a chunk,
referring to marinated chicken grilled to perfection before being mixed into the creamy, smoky, and aromatic sauce.

Originally from Northern India, tikka kebabs were traditionally cooked in tandoors over charcoal. The dish evolved into a fusion of Indian and
British cuisine, becoming a popular curry in the UK.

Ingredients:

  • 700 grams of boneless chicken
  • 120 ml of greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 tablepoon of lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon of musturd oil
  • 150 grams of Chopped onions
  • 500 grams of chooped tomatos
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream

Method:

Marination:

  1. In a bowl, combine ½ kg boneless chicken pieces with 1 tablespoon lemon juice and 1 tablespoon oil.
  2. Add ½ to 1 teaspoon red chili powder, 1 teaspoon garam masala, ½ teaspoon cumin powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, ¼ teaspoon turmeric, and ½ teaspoon salt.
  3. Mix well to coat the chicken evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight for better flavor.

Preparing the Tikka:

  1. Preheat your oven to 240°C (460°F) or prepare your grill.
  2. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers.
  3. Place the skewers on a baking tray lined with aluminum foil or on the grill.
  4. Brush the chicken with some oil to prevent sticking.
  5. Bake or grill for 9-10 minutes.
  6. Turn the skewers over, brush with oil again, and cook for another 9-10 minutes until slightly charred.
  7. Remove from the oven or grill and set aside

Making the Masala Gravy:

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add 1 bay leaf, 2 green cardamoms, 2 cloves, and a small piece of cinnamon stick. Sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.
  3. Add 1 cup finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Stir in 1½ teaspoons ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add 1 to 2 chopped green chilies and sauté for another minute.
  6. Add 1 cup tomato purée and cook until the oil separates from the masala followed by red chilli powder while mixing and cooking for 2-3minutes.
  7. Pour in 1 cup water and bring the gravy to a boil, reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add the grilled chicken pieces to the gravy, proceed to stir until the chicken is well coated.
  9. Cover and simmer for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  10. Stir in ¼ to ½ cup fresh cream and mix thoroughly.
  11. Season with salt to taste and mix well.
  12. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  13. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
